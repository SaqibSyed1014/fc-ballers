FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about FC Ballers.

Culture & Expectations

Culture Questions

What makes FC Ballers different?
A disciplined culture, individualized performance focus, fluid positional play, and strong accountability.
Do you accept beginners?
We accept players serious about improving and committed to the work.
How are starters chosen?
Based on form, consistency, performance, and tactical needs.
Tryouts

Tryouts Questions

What should I bring to tryouts?
Bring cleats, training gear, and water. Come ready to compete.
Is the tryout fee refundable?
No. All tryout payments are final to prevent last-minute no-shows.
What does the tryout fee cover?
Field rental, equipment, preparation, and administrative setup.
Can I try out if I’ve never played club or high school soccer?
Yes. Work ethic and discipline matter more than past experience.
Is there an age requirement?
Players must be 18 or older.
Team Structure

Team Structure Questions

How many players will be selected?
We carry a roster of 18–22 players depending on the season.
Do players get guaranteed playing time?
No one is guaranteed playing time. It’s earned through consistency and performance.
What league do you compete in?
League placement and schedule will be announced closer to kickoff.
Is there a women’s team?
A women’s division is planned for the future.
Training

Training Questions

How often does the team train?
Training schedules vary by season, but players should expect 1–3 team sessions weekly.
Are players required to train outside of practice?
Yes. Players follow individualized training and recovery plans through approved apps.
Where are practices held?
Typically in the NW Chicago suburban area. Exact locations announced weekly.
Admin & Logistics

Admin Questions

Are there player fees?
Yes — but only a one-time payment for the full season. FC Ballers covers all major costs, including: • League fees • Referee fees • Field rentals • Uniforms • Travel expenses for away games Players pay once and never deal with surprise fees.
